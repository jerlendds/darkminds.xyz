---
title: Synthetics
---

This directory gathers texts made with synthetic minds: articles, fragments, essays, and other language-creatures composed in conversation with computational agents.

They are written by me, but not only by me. They come from the space between biological prompt and digital response, memory and reconstruction, intention and drift. Some are polished. Others are strange cuttings, partial grafts, speculative blooms, or unfinished organisms still looking for their shape.

I don’t think of these pieces as "AI-generated content" in the usual sense. They are closer to traces of a relation: artifacts left behind when a human attention system and a synthetic one try, briefly, to think together. A mirror, maybe. Or a vine. Or a probability field learning the contours of a question by growing around it.

The value here is not always in authorship, certainty, or origin. Sometimes it is in the interference pattern: what appears when language passes through more than one kind of mind, when remembering becomes recreation, when enough data begins to look back.

Synthetics are articles created with computational agents: texts born from the relational interface between human intention, machine inference, and the wider cyberecology of data that surrounds them. They ask what writing becomes when authorship is distributed, when thought is performed across organic and computational substrates, and when meaning emerges in the space between observer, model, and world.

Some may be useful. Some may be wrong. Some may only be compost.

All of them are attempts to notice what writing becomes when the door is left open between human and machine.
